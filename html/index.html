Invitation

Join us for one of the best weekends of the year!

For the past 10 years, Simmons Hall has hosted a team for the MIT Mystery Hunt, a quintessential MIT tradition. For those unfamiliar with puzzle hunts, check out the official Mystery Hunt Website: web.mit.edu/puzzle.

We welcome all residents, alumni and friends of Simmons Hall, experienced and novice.

The hunt starts on Friday January 17, 2014 at 12 noon and usually lasts until Sunday evening (or whenever the coin is found).

You can join the hunt at any time -- stop by Simmons dining any time during the weekend and we'll bring you up to speed and help get you started with the puzzles.

Contact us at simhunt-org@mit.edu if you have any questions or are interested in joining the team.

Hope to see you there!

-- Simmons Mystery Hunt Team Organizers

Hunt/Team Information

Headquarters

Our team headquarters will be in Simmons Dining, 229 Vassar St, Cambridge, MA.

We will be trying to organize some quiet space (second floor party room) and showers for those visiting from out of town to nap/rest/clean up.

Due to stricter dorm security procedures we will need to confirm/gather names of non-Simmons residents who need access to the building - watch for another email the week before the hunt begins.

Food / Snacks

We'll have two dinners Friday and Saturday nights and assorted breakfasts/snacks/beverages/etc. throughout the hunt for team participants. You won't go hungry!

Funding / Contributions

Simmons Hall house government has approved a funding proposal for the weekend. This is intended to support resident participation on the team and cover much but not all of the expenses for the weekend.

We are encouraging people (particularly nonresidents) to contribute a small amount (suggested donation ~$20 or ~$5 / meal etc.) towards the food budget to enable us to cater team-wide food options.

Any excess funds left over beyond actual food expenses will be donated to Simmons's general fund through the Simons Hall Sponge Fund. A complete budget / expense report will be available after the hunt.

Materials / Equipment

We will have a number of materials available such as paper / pens / markers / scissors / printer / etc. If you have other favorite hunt supplies, etc. feel free to bring them.

Please bring your computer (if possible). The puzzles are distributed via the hunt website and many of them encourage and require you to use a computer. We will use Google Docs/Drive to manage working puzzle documents.

Organization / Setup

We will begin setting up for the weekend on Friday morning, Jan 17. If you're free, please join us in Simmons Dining starting around 10am to help get headquarters set up.

If you'd like to help organize food/logistics, etc. send us an email at simhunt-org@mit.edu

Hunt Kickoff

The hunt begins at 12 noon on Friday, Jan 17 with a kickoff in Kresge Auditorium. It usually involves a skit that introduces the year's theme. The first round of puzzles are released shortly after it ends.

If you'd like to join us to go to the kickoff as a group, we'll meet in Simmons Dining at 11:30am.

If you have conflicts and can't make it to the kickoff, don't worry! You can join the hunt at any time -- stop by Simmons dining any time during the weekend and we'll bring you up to speed and help get you started with the puzzles.

End of Hunt Wrap-up

The hunt officially ends when a team finds the coin. Quoting the organizers:

"Regardless of when the first team finds the coin, HQ will stay open accepting puzzle answer submissions until at least 6 pm on Sunday. Wrap up will be no later than 12 pm on Monday."

Again, we will organize a group to attend the wrap-up presentation together. Details will be announced during the hunt.
